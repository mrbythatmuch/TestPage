Notification

When an injury occurs and medical attention is beyond first aid or the injury requires hospitalization (i.e. OSHA Reportable), the following must be done:

1) Notify the Vice President of Customer Services immediately by text or phone call. The District Service Manager (DSM) must send a text message to the following personnel: VP of Customer Services, Directors of Operations (International and Americas), MD, RSM, HSSE WW Manager and local HSSE Regional Manager. Also, the DSM must follow up with an email to provide more incident details. At this point, Medical Personnel will be added to the e-mail distribution providing advance notice of the injury and impending notice of an INI.

2) An INI must be put into the IMS system same day as the injury occurs. This will notify the local team, Medical and WW HSSE Manager. If the injured person is not able to submit the initial INI, a third person must be designated to enter the information on behalf of the injured person.

3) Medical Department personnel must categorize the injury within 24 hours after the initial INI report was submitted.

4) The DSM should classify the INI no later than the day after the injury and preferably same day.

5) Target to complete the investigation will be 5 days after the INI report was first submitted. There may be instances where it is not possible to complete the investigation in 5 days. However, the investigator should have at least a preliminary investigation completed. If, for any reason, the investigation will not be completed in 5 days, the investigator must advise his/her manager and HSSE in order to provide adequate resources and ensure that the investigation is completed.
